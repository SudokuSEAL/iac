About Us

The University of Washington's Industrial Assessment Center is comprised of experienced UW faculty, staff, and students. Our multidisciplinary team of undergraduate and graduate students have varying interests, most specializing in engineering fields such as mechanical, environmental chemical, electrical, and computer engineering, as well as atmospheric and computer science. We are one of 28 other universities across the nation involved in the IAC program.

Why We Need the IAC

From both an environmental and economic perspective, one of the most critical issues we currently face is the industrial sector's inefficient use of energy. Conserving energy is vitally important, as it can help us generate less waste, keep the environment healthy and save a significant amount of money.

At IAC, we believe that managing industrial energy is a responsibility shared by the entire industrial community. To achieve a greater level of energy efficiency, it is vital that local manufacturers and small businesses engage in energy-friendly practices. Continuing in the tradition of the Energy Analysis and Diagnostic Centers, our team of faculty and students provide a valuable service to the local economy and environment.

Our Mission

Our team offers free energy and waste assessments to qualifying facilities in Washington state. We provide excellent feedback and recommendations for both the environmental and economic aspects of your case. Through our efforts, your company will benefit from increased savings and energy efficiency, and we will see our students become the next generation of energy-savvy engineers.

Our Contributions

  • Achieving Environmental and Economic Goals

    Our energy, productivity, and waste assessments are completely free. Funding is provided by the U.S Department of Energy, which allows us to focus on the goals of our clients. We are determined to help you keep your bottom line thriving, and the environment thriving with it.

  • Quality Training to the Industry's New Generation

    We have coached more than 40 students since the establishment of UW IAC, and are always on the lookout for new talent. Our students join the workforce with solid knowledge about the inner workings of industrial facilities, both the mechanical components, and the business and logistical side to running a good plant. They gain workable experience and innovative skills in their time with us. We are always on the lookout for new talent.
